Priority Trades

Across 3 tiers spanning CDS, bonds, puts, and exchange-traded instruments.

Bond spreads cost 1–2% annually to carry versus equity dividends of 10–18%. Duration leverage amplifies spread moves into large price changes. Ratings cliff dynamics create forced-selling cascades that do not exist in equity markets. These trades are less crowded as most participants focus on equity shorts.

Three Highest-Conviction Trades
1

Athene 5yr CDS ~1% carry

Cleanest expression, no bond borrow needed, isolates pure credit risk. NAIC reform catalyst with defined 2026–2028 timeline. 15–20% upside in stress.

2

Short CRBD on Nasdaq + Long TLT ~1.9% carry

Exchange-traded bond shortable through any equity brokerage. 40yr maturity = ~15yr modified duration. Market prices CRBG as vanilla insurer; it is functionally a PE-insurer nexus ($70bn Blackstone, $90bn BlackRock, $24bn Carlisle).

3

Short BPCE Tier 2 + Long Bunds ~1.5% carry

Most mispriced instrument in entire thesis. No equity analyst coverage (cooperative). Bond analysts treat as generic French bank credit. Natixis sits on virtually every BDC facility. Zero PC risk premium in spread.

⚠ One to Avoid

GBDC equity short — Thesis is correct (26% software, dividend cut) but ~14–16% annual carry from dividend is punishing. Need GBDC to fall >15% in year one just to break even on carry.

Key Structural Insight: Ratings cliff dynamics in BDC bonds (e.g. FSK) create forced-selling cascades from IG mandates that do not exist in equity markets. A single-notch downgrade from BBB- to BB+ can trigger 10–15 points of price movement in days, far exceeding the equivalent equity reaction.
