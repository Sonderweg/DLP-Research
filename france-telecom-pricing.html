Executive Summary

The French telecom market (Jan 2023 – Jan 2026) shows a clear bifurcation: premium plans are diverging sharply in strategy while entry-level plans converge to near-identical pricing. Orange is pushing aggressive upselling (+€20 on premium mobile), SFR has restructured away from high headline SIM-only, Free holds its disruptive €19.99 anchor, and Bouygues treads a cautious middle path.

Key Market Dynamics

Fixed broadband shows remarkable convergence: entry fibre dropped from €28–€40 to ~€24–€25 across all operators. The speed race ended with universal 8 Gbps by late 2024. Mobile sub-brands (B&You, Sosh, RED) have converged at €20–€21 for top plans and €1.99 for entry, eliminating meaningful differentiation at the low end.

Investment Implications — Mobile

  • Orange's premium push is a bet on ARPU growth — monitor churn closely
  • SFR's restructuring signals margin pressure; watch for further simplification
  • Free's €19.99 anchor is structural — any move would be a major market signal
  • Entry-level convergence at €1.99 means zero differentiation below the line
  • Sub-brand pricing (~€20) is now a commodity — value creation must come from bundling

Investment Implications — Fixed

  • Premium fibre is stable (€45–€58 range) — limited pricing risk
  • Entry fibre convergence at ~€25 signals a mature, commoditised segment
  • Speed race complete (8 Gbps universal) — no more differentiation via speed
  • Free's premium increase (€49.99→€59.99) is notable: testing price elasticity
  • Next battlefield: content bundling, smart home, and convergent offers

Competitive Dynamics

  • Consolidation pressure: pricing convergence makes standalone economics harder
  • Value migration: from SIM-only to device-bundled plans (esp. Orange, SFR)
  • Sub-brand squeeze: B&You, Sosh, RED now nearly interchangeable on price
  • Infrastructure monetisation: operators shifting to wholesale/infrastructure plays

Risks to Watch

  • Regulatory intervention if entry prices creep back up
  • Free breaking the €19.99 anchor (upside: ARPU re-rating; downside: price war)
  • New MVNO entrants exploiting the sub-brand gap
  • 5G standalone rollout costs impacting capex discipline
